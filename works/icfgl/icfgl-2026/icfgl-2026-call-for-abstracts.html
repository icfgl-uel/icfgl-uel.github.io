Conference Rationale

Feminist law-making can be defined as a legal methodology aimed at reforming the conceptual framework and patriarchal foundations of legal systems by rethinking and applying the law from feminist perspectives. It seeks to deconstruct the contemporary legal structure to expose its patriarchal, male-centric underlying ideologies.

The 2026 ICFGL adopts feminist law-making as its main theme, focusing on this methodology as an alternative to both gender mainstreaming and critical feminism. We invite scholars to move from standing “before the law” as outside critics to engaging “with the law” as active agents who appropriate and reconfigure legal tools.

Conference Themes

Theme 1

Feminist Legal History & "Inconspicuous Impact"

Exploring the history of feminist legal advocacy and the often-overlooked impacts of women's movements. Do feminist efforts need immediate legislative results to be successful?

Theme 2

Re-evaluating the Public/Private Divide

Focusing on how the law can transcend the public/private divide to revalue the private sphere of family, domestic labour, and intimate relationships.

Theme 3

"Sceptical Pragmatism" & Legal Mobilization

Can the law, with its patriarchal foundations, effectively erase inequality? How to maintain subversive goals without reproducing patriarchal norms?

Theme 4

Intersectionality & Marginalized Voices

How legislative frameworks accommodate diverse identities (LGBT+, minority groups) and incorporate "lived experiences" into policy-making.

Theme 5

How to Actually Write Feminist Laws

Exploring ways to write laws that directly incorporate stories and daily realities of women, rather than just abstract legal norms.

Theme 6

Imagine the Law Differently (DIY Law)

Writing legal texts and judgments "as if" women's voices and equality were already fully respected and authorized.

Submission Requirements

  • Publication Opportunity: Selected papers considered for Griffith Law Review Special Issue.
  • Format: Microsoft Word preferred.
