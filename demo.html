Flagship Stream

Towards a Vietnamese
Feminist Judgment

A flagship stream of the project that adapts feminist judgment methodology to the Vietnamese legal context. This initiative explores rewritten judicial decisions that highlight gendered perspectives and structural inequalities.

In 2022, we launched the "Feminist Judgments" project with the goal of contributing to the global discourse on feminist legal research. We were privileged to receive the mentorship of Professor Rosemary Hunter, a pioneer in this field. Through seminars and guidance on methodology and philosophy, we published the foundational book "Towards a Vietnamese Feminist Judgment" in late 2023, with Volume II expected in 2026.

The project represents a crucial adaptation to a local context where alternative judgment writing faces unique challenges. While international projects often pair a judge with an academic, the Vietnamese landscape requires scholars to bridge this gap by performing both roles.

National Student Competition

Gender Justice
Moot Court

The Gender Justice Moot Court is a national-level competition for undergraduate law students across Vietnam. It provides a structured and practice-oriented platform for developing legal research, written advocacy, and oral argumentation skills, while integrating gender justice principles into legal reasoning.
